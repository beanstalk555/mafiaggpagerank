Rules

 Violation of any rule may result in a ban, though you may receive a warning first.
  1. Full disclosure - I'm an academic, and my motivation for doing all of this is to conduct research on the game of mafia. By participating in this game, you consent to your in-game logs being collected and to your username and win/loss data appearing in these public rankings. You also consent to the use of your data to develop mafia AI. If this makes you uncomfortable, please do not play.
  2. Be kind and respectful to other players, especially new players. This includes name calling and personal insults. I know games get heated, but it's just a game in the end, and everyone deserves to have fun. Note that there is a big difference between "that was dumb" (probably fine) and "you are dumb" (not fine). Additionally, hate speech of any kind is not tolerated.
  3. Don't cheat. In particular, no multi-accounting, and no communication with any players outside of the game while the game is running.
  4. Fake AFKing (not talking or voting during the day, but clicking the button to stay in the game at night) is not allowed.
  5. Using hydras (multiple people playing on the same account at the same time) is not allowed.
  6. Play to your win condition to the best of your ability. In particular, do not purposely throw games, and do not leave games while you are alive except in case of emergency. AFKs will be autobanned at the end of each game.
  7. Anything else I missed from https://mafia.gg/guide/behavior
  8. Keep spectator chat spoiler-free. If you were a player in the game, please do not discuss the game at all in spectator chat while it is still running.
  9. Say "condemn" instead of "lynch." In the United States and other countries, "lynch" is a charged word associated with heinous acts of violence and hate against historically and currently marginalized groups. Therefore it has strong potential to make members of these groups and others feel uncomfortable, unwelcome, or even unsafe.

About PageRank

 PageRank is an algorithm developed by Google to rank the importance of web pages based on their outgoing links to other pages, but it has numerous other applications. It creates a directed graph with a node associated to each page, and arrows between nodes associated to links from one page to another. Using the graph's adjacency matrix, it (more-or-less) simulates a random walk in this graph and assigns a weight to each node, where the weight roughly stands for the proportion of time that the walk is visiting that node.

To calculate mafia rankings using PageRank, I use a graph with a node for each user who has ever used the system. For each game, I consider each (winner, loser) pair and add an arrow from the loser to the winner (or increase the arrow's weight if it already exists). To stabilize things a bit and help deal with sinks in the graph, I also add weighted edges from each node to itself.

A higher score is because it correlates with lots of incoming arrows (wins against other players). However, it isn't just your wins that matter, but who you win against. It sounds paradoxical, but your rank is higher if you have wins against higher-ranked players.

Modified PageRank

After a few games, I noticed that PageRank heavily rewards players who have a perfect win record, even if they have only played one game. I want to encourage people to play more, even if they lose. Thus, I created a modified version of PageRank which factors in how many games each player has played as compared to the average. This weighting factor is then averaged (currently, with a dampened weighting factor of 1/5) with PageRank to create the rankings in the Modified PageRank and Modified Rank columns.
